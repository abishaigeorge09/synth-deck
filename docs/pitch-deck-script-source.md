# synth. Pitch Deck Script Source

This document captures the current content, narrative flow, and supporting proof points from the main `synth.` pitch deck. It is meant to help write a spoken pitch script that matches the slides as they exist today.

## Deck Purpose

The deck is trying to do four things in sequence:

1. Make the coaching problem feel urgent and real.
2. Position `synth.` as the missing synthesis layer across fragmented athlete data.
3. Show that the product is real and that there is early pull.
4. End with a credible wedge, a believable expansion story, and a specific ask.

## Core Story Spine

- Coaches and athletes already do the work to collect data, but decisions are still made with incomplete information.
- The problem is not just "too many tools." The real consequence is worse decisions, missed fatigue/recovery signals, and lower performance.
- Rowing is the wedge because the pain is acute, the workflows are data-heavy, and the team has direct credibility there.
- `synth.` is positioned as the synthesis layer, not another isolated tool.
- The wedge starts in rowing, but the company ambition is to expand into every sport with fragmented athlete-data workflows.

## Slide-by-Slide Script Source

### Slide 1: Title

**Section:** `TITLE`

**What the slide says**

- Brand: `synth.`
- Tagline: `Every data signal. One platform.`

**What the slide is doing**

- This is a brand-first opening.
- It introduces the company as a unified layer before any detailed product claim.

**Speaker intent**

- Open confidently and simply.
- Set up the big idea that all the signals already exist, but they are not currently unified into one usable system.

**Best transition**

- Move from brand promise into the real-world pain that makes the promise matter.

### Slide 2: Problem Statements

**Section:** `01 · PROBLEM`

**What the slide says**

- When coaches decide with incomplete info, athlete performance drops.
- With `120 athletes` on Cal Rowing, fatigue and recovery are hard to spot early.
- They collect the data, but it still does not tell them `what to do next` fast.
- Data lives in `8+ tools`, so there is no single complete view.

**What the slide is doing**

- It turns the problem into simple, human language.
- It makes the consequence explicit: the issue is not just messy software, it is weaker coaching decisions and lower athlete performance.

**Speaker intent**

- Keep this practical, not technical.
- Emphasize that all the data work is already happening, but the output is still not actionable enough.

**Key proof point**

- `120 athletes` at Cal Rowing
- `8+ tools`

**Best transition**

- After naming the pain, show the actual fragmented environment visually.

## Recurring Themes To Use In The Script

### The problem is decision quality, not software inconvenience

Keep returning to this. The strongest emotional version of the story is:

- fragmented data leads to incomplete coaching decisions
- incomplete decisions lead to missed fatigue and recovery signals
- that ultimately affects performance

### Rowing is the wedge, not the ceiling

The deck consistently uses rowing and Cal as the starting point, then broadens into a cross-sport infrastructure opportunity.

### `8+ tools` is a core memory hook

This appears in both the problem and solution story and is one of the easiest numbers for an audience to remember.

### `synth.` is the synthesis layer

This is the central product positioning:

- not another isolated app
- not just a dashboard
- not just a data pipe
- the missing layer that unifies signals and produces action

### Founder credibility matters

The team story works best when it supports product insight, not when it is just a biography section.

### Timing is a 2025 inflection

The `Why Now` slide creates urgency by showing:

- rising athlete data volume
- rising athlete spend per program
- rising disconnected tools per staff

That gives you a strong "why now, why us, why this category" bridge.

## Recommended Script Structure

If you want to turn this into a spoken pitch, the strongest flow is:

1. Open with the one-line company idea.
2. Make the pain concrete in coaching terms.
3. Broaden it into a system-wide category problem.
4. Explain `synth.` as the missing synthesis layer.
5. Show the product.
6. Prove early traction.
7. Show why the timing is right now.
8. Show the market and wedge.
9. Explain why no one else fully solves this.
10. Show founder credibility.
11. Expand into long-term vision.
12. End with a direct ask.

## Script-Writing Notes

- Keep the spoken version simpler than the slide text.
- Use the slides as proof, not as a teleprompter.
- Avoid reading all numbers aloud unless they strengthen conviction.
- On chart and market slides, focus on the conclusion, not every datapoint.
- On the product demo slide, narrate only the key moments.
- On traction, verify any placeholder-backed metrics before a live investor conversation.
